Parasnath Hill - Giridih

Famous for its scenery and deep cultural & religious importance.

Parasnath Hill, located in Jharkhandâ€™s Giridih district, is one of the most important religious and tourist destinations in eastern India. It is revered as a sacred site by Jains, as it is believed to be the place where twenty of the twenty-four Jain Tirthankaras attained moksha (salvation).

Apart from its immense spiritual significance, the hill is also known for its scenic beauty, lush greenery, and pleasant climate, making it a popular destination for nature lovers, trekkers, and pilgrims alike. The journey to the top of Parasnath Hill offers not only a serene spiritual experience but also mesmerizing views of the surrounding forests and valleys.

Highlights:

  • Visitors can pray at the Shikharji Temple at the summit, one of the holiest Jain pilgrimage sites.
  • Visitors can learn history from ancient Jain temples and shrines spread along the pathway.
  • It offers peaceful environment, ideal for meditation and spiritual retreats.
  • Best time to visit: From October to March.
  • Especially avoid the monsoon season (July to September), when the trails may become slippery due to heavy rainfall.
