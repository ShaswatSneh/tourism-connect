Maa Dewri Temple - Tamar

Famous for its ancient architecture, historical legends & wish fulfillment beliefs.

The Maa Dewri Mandir is most famous for its extraordinary idol of Goddess Durga, a unique representation where the deity is depicted with 16 arms (Solah Bhuji), each holding a different weapon. This ancient idol, believed to date back 700 years, is a rare find and the central focus of devotion.

Spiritually, the temple serves as a major pilgrimage site for wish-fulfillment; devotees practice a unique ritual of tying red and yellow threads on a bamboo stick to make a wish, returning to untie them once their prayers are answered. Situated on the scenic NH33 highway near Ranchi, the temple is not just a place of worship but a confluence of history, faith, and local legend, attracting thousands of visitors and devotees every year.

Highlights:

  • Visitors can offer prayers and prasad, participate in rituals and perform special ceremonies.
  • A popular ritual involves tying a red and yellow thread on a bamboo structure on the temple premises to make a wish. Once the wish is fulfilled, devotees return to untie it as a sign of gratitude.
  • Near the temple is the famous Sun Temple, featuring an ornate design in the shape of a large chariot.
  • The serene and peaceful environment of the temple's location offers an opportunity for quiet reflection and appreciating the natural surroundings.
